## Reflection (150–200 words)

For this task, I created accounts on GitHub, LinkedIn, and Kaggle, three platforms that are essential for students in computer science and engineering. GitHub is a collaborative code-hosting platform, and I set up my profile with my name, branch, year, and a short introduction about my learning goals. This will serve as my coding portfolio throughout my degree, where I plan to upload projects and assignments. LinkedIn is a professional networking site widely used by employers and industry professionals. I added my education details, including my current degree at VIT Bhopal, my branch, and my expected graduation year, so that my profile reflects my academic journey. Kaggle is a global data science community where learners can practise machine learning and data analysis. I chose Kaggle because data-driven skills are becoming increasingly important across all engineering fields.

The most valuable part of this task was realizing how each platform supports a different aspect of professional growth: GitHub for coding, LinkedIn for networking, and Kaggle for analytics. Over the next four years, I plan to keep updating GitHub with semester projects, expand my LinkedIn connections, and participate in Kaggle challenges to strengthen my analytical and problem-solving skills.
