# Task 1 – Digital Literacy Infographic

## Tool Used
**Canva** (canva.com) – Free online design platform

## Design Link

## What My Infographic Shows
The infographic covers three main topics:
1. **What is Digital Literacy** – Definition and importance for students
2. **Professional Online Presence** – Maintaining a clean, credible digital identity
3. **Safe Internet Practices** – Using strong passwords, recognising phishing, avoiding oversharing



## Reflection (150–200 words)

For this task, I chose Canva to design a one-page infographic on digital literacy for first-year students. Canva’s simple drag-and-drop features made it easy to combine text, icons, and color blocks without needing advanced design skills. My infographic is organized into three clear sections: a definition of digital literacy, practical tips for safe internet use, and guidance on building a professional online presence. I used a clean blue-and-white theme to keep the slide visually appealing and easy to read.
The most engaging part of the process was deciding which topics to highlight. Digital literacy covers a wide range of skills, so narrowing it down to just three forced me to think carefully about what would benefit new students the most. The biggest challenge was balancing clarity with detail—making sure the text was informative but not overwhelming. Through this, I realized that effective design is about focus: a simple, well-structured message often has more impact than trying to include everything at once.
