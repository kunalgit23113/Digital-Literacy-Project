## Reflection (150–200 words)

For this task, I explored two different types of platforms: one for coding practice and another for collaborative communication. On HackerRank, I created an account and attempted the introductory Python challenge. The platform provides a structured environment to practise programming in multiple domains such as Python, SQL, and algorithms. Even as a beginner, I found the interface simple to use and the problem statements clear. Completing my first challenge gave me confidence and showed me how I can gradually build programming skills over the course of my degree.

For the collaboration component, I designed a five‑question Digital Literacy Awareness Quiz using Google Forms. The quiz included both multiple‑choice and short‑answer questions, and responses were automatically collected in a linked Google Sheet. This tool is practical because I can share the form with classmates to check their awareness, which reflects the kind of real‑world activity expected from a Student Digital Ambassador.

Together, these platforms highlight how technical skill development and collaborative communication can go hand in hand. HackerRank strengthens my coding abilities, while Google Forms supports teamwork and awareness building within my batch. Both will be valuable resources throughout my academic journey.
