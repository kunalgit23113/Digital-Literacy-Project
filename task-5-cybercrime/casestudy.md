# Task 5 – Cybercrime Case Study

## Cybercrime Type: Phishing

---

## Case Study: The Scholarship Email That Wasn't

### Background
In 2023, several university students across India received emails claiming to be from the National Scholarship Portal (scholarships.gov.in). The emails stated that the students' scholarship payments were on hold due to a "verification issue" and that they must re-enter their banking details within 48 hours to avoid losing the funds.

### How It Happened (Step-by-Step)

1. **Bait is crafted:** Attackers designed an email that closely mimicked the official NSP branding — logo, colour scheme, and formal language. The sender address appeared as `noreply@nsp-scholarship-verify.in` (not the real government domain).

2. **Urgency is created:** The email warned of a 48-hour deadline to create panic and discourage careful reading.

3. **Victim clicks the link:** Students clicked a link that opened a fake website nearly identical to the real scholarship portal, complete with a login form.

4. **Credentials are entered:** Students entered their Aadhaar-linked bank account numbers, IFSC codes, and in some cases, OTPs they received (forwarded to attackers through SIM-swap tricks).

5. **Funds are stolen:** Within hours, money was transferred out of victims' accounts. Some students lost ₹8,000–₹15,000 — their entire semester stipend.

### Who Is Targeted
First-generation college students, rural students receiving government aid, and anyone unfamiliar with how government portals communicate — typically they do not send urgent emails asking for banking details.

### Consequences
- Direct financial loss
- Emotional distress and loss of trust in digital systems
- Difficulty getting money back (UPI/bank transfers are often irreversible)
- Loss of time dealing with complaints and bank disputes
