# Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist
### For College Students in India

---

## 🛡️ General Online Safety

- [ ] **1. Verify the sender's email address carefully** – Check the full domain, not just the display name. Real government portals (like NIC, NSP) use `.gov.in` domains. Any variation (e.g., `.in`, `.org`, `-verify.in`) is a red flag.

- [ ] **2. Never click links in unsolicited emails or SMS** – Go directly to the official website by typing the URL in your browser. Bookmark official sites like scholarships.gov.in, vitbhopal.ac.in, and irctc.co.in.

- [ ] **3. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)** – Turn on 2FA for your Gmail, GitHub, LinkedIn, and any banking apps. Even if your password is stolen, attackers cannot access your account without the second code.

- [ ] **4. Use strong, unique passwords** – Do not use your name, date of birth, or "123456". Use a passphrase like `BlueMountain@VIT2024` or use a free password manager like Bitwarden.

- [ ] **5. Keep your devices and apps updated** – Software updates patch security vulnerabilities. Enable automatic updates on your phone and laptop.

- [ ] **6. Do not use public Wi-Fi for sensitive tasks** – Avoid logging into banking apps, entering OTPs, or accessing college portals on open/unprotected Wi-Fi networks (e.g., at cafes or bus stands).

---

## 💰 Financial & UPI Safety (Mandatory Tips)

- [ ] **7. Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or bank password with anyone** – No bank, government officer, or scholarship authority will ever ask for your PIN or OTP over call, SMS, or email. If someone asks, it is a scam — disconnect immediately.

- [ ] **8. Be alert to "receive money" UPI scam** – Fraudsters send a UPI collect request asking you to enter your PIN to "receive" money. Entering your PIN sends money TO them, not from them. You only enter your PIN when PAYING, never when receiving.

- [ ] **9. Verify before scanning QR codes** – Only scan QR codes from trusted, verified sources. Fraudsters place fake QR stickers over legitimate ones at shops, hostels, and canteens.

---

## 🆘 How to Report a Cybercrime

| Channel | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **National Cyber Crime Portal** | [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) – File a complaint online |
| **Cyber Crime Helpline** | **1930** – Available 24x7 for financial fraud reporting |
| **Your Bank** | Call your bank's helpline immediately to freeze a fraudulent transaction |
| **VIT Bhopal IT Cell** | Report any phishing emails targeting your VIT email ID to the IT department |

> ⚡ **Act fast:** For financial fraud, call **1930** or freeze your account within the first 30–60 minutes for the best chance of recovery.
