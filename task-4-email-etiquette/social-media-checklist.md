# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

## ✅ DO's

| # | Do This |
|---|---------|
| 1 | **Keep your profile professional** – Use a clear, appropriate photo and a neutral, informative bio. Employers and internship coordinators often check social media before shortlisting. |
| 2 | **Think before you post** – Ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my professor or a future employer saw this?" If not, don't post it. |
| 3 | **Use privacy settings** – Set personal accounts (Instagram, Snapchat) to private. Keep professional platforms (LinkedIn, GitHub) public and polished. |
| 4 | **Credit others' work** – If you share someone else's content, always tag or acknowledge the original creator. |
| 5 | **Report harassment** – If you witness or experience cyberbullying or offensive behaviour, use the platform's reporting tools. Do not engage with trolls. |
| 6 | **Separate personal and professional** – Keep your LinkedIn focused on academics and career. Reserve casual content for close-friends-only personal accounts. |
| 7 | **Verify before sharing** – Before sharing news or information, check if it comes from a credible, known source. Spreading misinformation — even unintentionally — can damage your reputation. |

---

## ❌ DON'Ts

| # | Avoid This |
|---|-----------|
| 1 | **Don't share personal contact details publicly** – Avoid posting your phone number, home address, college room number, or daily schedule on public accounts. |
| 2 | **Don't post in anger or frustration** – Emotional posts made in haste are hard to take back and can harm relationships and future opportunities. Draft it, wait 24 hours, then reconsider. |
| 3 | **Don't accept friend/follow requests from strangers** – Especially those with incomplete profiles, very few followers, or who immediately ask for personal information. |
| 4 | **Don't share your location in real time** – Posting "at the mall right now" or checking in publicly tells people you are away from your room and reveals your location to unknown followers. |
| 5 | **Don't plagiarise or misrepresent yourself** – Don't list skills, certifications, or achievements you don't have on LinkedIn. Dishonesty discovered during recruitment ends opportunities immediately. |
| 6 | **Don't engage in or forward cyberbullying** – Even forwarding a humiliating message about someone makes you a participant. Digital harassment has serious academic and legal consequences. |
| 7 | **Don't use college/official accounts for personal opinions** – If your email or account identifies you as a VIT student, it represents the institution. Keep controversial opinions off it. |
